Getting the whole lab to Seoul for ICML week was chaotic in the best way — badge printers jamming, someone's poster tube going missing at baggage claim, and a group chat that would not stop buzzing. Worth every bit of it.

The poster session for our KV-cache work ran long, in a good way: a steady stream of people stopping to ask pointed questions, a few of whom turned into genuinely useful conversations about failure cases we hadn't fully written up.

![Standing next to the poster during the session](/images/gallery/icml-2026-qtale-poster.jpg)

A few things that stuck with me after the week:

- Reviewer questions at the poster are a much better editor than any internal rehearsal.
- The best ideas this week came from hallway conversations, not talks.
- Next year, print the poster a day earlier.

Already looking forward to writing this one up properly for the lab wiki — and to next year's trip.
